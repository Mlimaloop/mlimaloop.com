1 The collector

It starts with
one bottle, and
one person.

A collector picks up a bottle from a roadside in Arusha. She scans it into the MlimaLoop app, drops it at a buying station, and is paid instantly, fairly, transparently, every single time.

  • $0.165 / kg, paid on the spot through the app, no middleman, no waiting.
  • A dedicated collector earns around $210 a month, roughly 3× Tanzania's minimum wage.
  • Every kilo is GPS-stamped and named, the chain of custody that unlocks premium ocean-bound plastic credits.

The surplus is already growing.

Feedstock is being collected and stored on site right now, bought at $0.165/kg and already worth $0.40/kg at today's market price. The stockpile grows every single day, an appreciating asset on the balance sheet before the plant even switches on.

Inside the plant

From bottle to food-grade pellet.

Because we stockpile feedstock during build-out, the plant runs two shifts from day one, around 7–8 tonnes processed daily. Six stages, fully traceable, all the way to a food-grade reactor and a pilot yarn line.

🗂️
01

Collect & sort

Named collectors, app-logged, sorted by resin and colour.

🔪
02

Shred to flake

Granulated, friction-washed, caps float off for PP recovery.

💧
03

Hot wash

Caustic hot-wash to food-contact cleanliness; water recycled.

⚙️
04

Extrude to pellet

Twin-screw extrusion into uniform, sellable rPET pellet.

🧪
05

SSP reactor

Solid-state upgrade to food-grade IV, the highest-value gate.

🧵
06

Pilot yarn

Sample yarn for brand qualification and the textile route.

Non-food pellet sells immediately. Food-grade follows once certified, commanding premium, long-term offtake from beverage and FMCG brands.
